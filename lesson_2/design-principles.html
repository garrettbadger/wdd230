Fitt's Law

Hoonigan

Hoonigan.com

The mobile site for Hoonigan was a great representation of Fitt's law. This the design principle that the smaller the target to either click or touch, the longer it will take for the user to make a decision. Hoonigan has a very large picture right on the front of their mobile page that cycles through their different products. This makes it quite easy to select what you want in a way that is convenient especially on a mobile touch screen.

White Space and Clean Design

Lamborghini

Lamborghini.com

Lamborghini's website was not cluttered at all and has a very clean design. It was easy to see that they let their products do most of the talking and didn't bother adding too much design that could distract the user. They utilize their whitespace very well. On the mobile version you can pretty much only see their use of micro whitespace just because it is a smaller screen size. Overall this site works very well on mobile and it not too claustrophobic.

PARC Repetition

Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints

churchofjesuschrist.org

The Church's website was a great example of one of the big four principles of design, repetition. They have multiple block elements that all follow a similar pattern. They have a picture as the background and toward the bottom is the title of what that element is about. Each element is separated by the same amount of white space and each element is the same size. It shows a uniformity that is pleasing to the user.
